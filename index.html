This page contains the list of minor planets which I am/was personally interested in. None of these were observed through my (miserable) equipment - they were found in open-access image data of professional observatories (unless otherwise noted).
T09 Subaru Telescope observations are by far the most numerous. Others are G96 Catalina Sky Survey and SOHO space telescope.


Green trajectories are the orbits of the first 9 entries in the table below. Dashed lines are the 8 major planets of the Solar System.

1 (697402) 2017 BX232 MBA 1/7 Yes The first numbered minor planet which I have sent the discovery observation for. A main-belt object in a pretty circular orbit with a semimajor axis of around 3 AU, and an inclination of around 8 deg.
2 2017 FG226 TNO 3/7 Probably The first trans-Neptunian object which I have reported, and the first object I have ever reported myself that has received a provisional designation.
3 2017 AC64 TNO/SDO 4/7 Probably A scattered disk object with a semimajor axis of 75 AU, an eccentricity of 0.46, reaching an aphelion of 110 AU. It also has an inclination of 28 degrees. The most distant and unusal minor planet I have reported so far.
4 2017 DW159 Centaur 4/7 Probably A centaur with an inclination of nearly 30 degrees. Passes outside of Uranus' orbit with a Uranus MOID of 1.5 AU, and goes beyond Neptune's orbit out to 36 AU.
5 2017 BM230 Jupiter Trojan 4/7 Probably A Jupiter trojan with an inclination of 34 degrees. The most inclined object I might have discovered.
6 2017 AD62 TNO 3/7 Probably A trans-Neptunian object with an inclination of about 24 degrees.
7 2017 DK163 TNO/SDO 4/7 Probably A scattered disc object with a semimajor axis of 68 AU, eccentricity of 0.46, reaching out to nearly 100 AU at aphelion. Neptune MOID of 8 AU.
8 2017 EE52 TNO 3/7 Probably A TNO for which I have sent an ITF-ITF identification.

* Type Abbreviations:

** HAG Catalog Remarkability Levels:
1: Unremarkable (typical MBA, objects later turned out to be artificial...)
2: Non-mundane (rare MBA...)
3: Interesting (typical TNO, NEO...)
4: Very Unusual (SDO, detached object, Atira, PHA...)
5: Extreme (Parabolic/Hyperbolic orbit, small impactor...)
6: Unique (Interstellar object, large impactor...)
7: World-Shattering (Existential crisis impactor, alien spacecraft...)
